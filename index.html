Quick Start Guide

Audio: Before using the Harmonagon, please adjust the volume on your device.

Full Screen: A large display monitor and browser in full screen mode are recommended for optimum user experience. Choose Enter Full Screen from the View menu or use the keyboard shortcuts for Windows (F11) or Mac OS (Command + Control + F).

Making music is as easy as 1-2-3…

  1. Click or tap the Make Chord or Make Scale button on the center console.
  2. Click or tap to select one or more notes, from 0 through 11, on the circle.
  3. Click or tap the Enter button to see and hear the interval, chord, or scale you’ve created.

Tip: When making chords, limit the number of notes you select from 2 to 4, or for chords with tensions, 5 or more. When making scales, select from 5 to 7 notes, or more for longer scales. And always try to enclose the center of the Harmonagon within the shapes you create. When shapes do not encompass or at least intersect with the Harmonagon center point, the chord or scale produced will in most cases return “undefined.”

Show/Hide: Click or tap the center of the Harmonagon to display or hide the center button console.

Clear: Click or tap the Clear button to reset the Harmonagon to the default state.

Play: Click or tap the Play button to play any interval, chord, or scale that is displayed.

Loop: Click or tap the Loop button to play along or practice with intervals, arpeggios, chords, and scales. Click or tap the middle of the screen or press the spacebar to stop the loop.

Change Tempo: Select Tempo from the Change menu and choose from 30 to 240 beats per minute (bpm). The default is 120 bpm.

Add Chord: With one interval or chord created, click or tap the Add Chord button to create a second interval or chord.

Swap Chords: With two intervals or chords created, click or tap the Swap Chords button to switch between chords.

Loop Chords: With two intervals or chords created, click or tap the Loop Chords button to loop a sequence of the two chords. Click or tap the middle of the screen to cancel and stop playing the loop.

Merge: With two intervals or chords created, click or tap the Merge button to combine the chords into a matching scale. Click or tap Undo Merge to restore the two chords.

Learn: Choose any Chord, Scale, or Sequence from the Learn menu to instantly see and hear the selection presented and played on the Harmonagon.

Watch: Introduction to the Harmonagon 4-minute video.

IMPORTANT: The Harmonagon is updated frequently with new and improved features, so select Reload on the menu each time you visit to assure you are using the most current version.

System Requirements: The Harmonagon runs client-side on desktop or laptop computers, tablets or smart phones with a modern web browser, such as Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Explorer, and an Internet/Wi-Fi connection or cell signal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use the Harmonagon?

It’s as easy as 1-2-3.

  1. Click or tap the Make Chord or Make Scale button on the center console.
  2. Click or tap to select one or more notes, from 0 through 11, on the circle.
  3. Click or tap the Enter button to see and hear the interval, chord, or scale you’ve created.

Please read the Quick Start Guide for an overview of app features.

Are there any tutorial videos for the Harmonagon?

Yes. The Harmonagon includes a program of videos and quizzes for self-paced learning. Our series of free music theory lessons includes an introductory video, an overview of the app's features, a Notes and Steps video and then a quiz, with more quizzes and videos coming soon.

Subscribe to our YouTube channel and follow us on Facebook and Twitter for notification of new videos, quizzes, and program features.

What new features are planned for the Harmonagon?

There are many features in development, including:

  • Cross-Referencing of Chords and Scales,
  • Change Key or Tonic,
  • Invert or Change Register,
  • Transpose Chords and Scales,
  • Transform Chords and Scales,
  • Change Scale Mode,
  • Change Chord Root or Use Alternate Bass,
  • Change Instrument Sound,
  • Make and Save Sequences and Chord Progressions,
  • Metronome and Tuner,
  • Rhythm Mode,
  • Education Levels for Preschool and Elementary Students,
  • and more.

The Harmonagon is updated often with new and improved functions so select Reload from the menu to refresh your browser each time you visit to assure that you are using the most recent release. Follow us on Facebook or Twitter and check the Harmonagon Project's YouTube Channel.

The Harmonagon™ tradename is an amalgam of the words harmony and polygon.

The Harmonagon is not working properly, what should I do?

If you have a poor cell signal or slow Wi-Fi/Internet connection, it may take a few seconds for the Harmonagon app and sound files to completely load.

The Harmonagon is updated frequently with new features and improvements, so so select Reload from the menu to refresh your browser each time you visit to ensure that you are using the most current and up-to-date version of the app.

To reload and bypass your browser’s cache: Chrome, Firefox, and Explorer users hold the Shift key when clicking Reload to force reload the page; or use the keyboard shortcut: Command + Shift + R; Safari users hold the Command key and click the Reload button. Chrome users still experiencing problems should select Clear Browsing Data… from the Chrome menu and clear Cached images and files and Hosted app data.

Hint: Click the app icon left of the Create menu or Reload to refresh the page.

About The Harmonagon

The Harmonagon™ is a new web app that uses simple geometric shapes to teach music to anyone, anywhere, for free.

The app is primarily designed as a learning and practice tool for music students and musicians, but anyone can use it, including beginners and those without an instrument.

For more information, check out the FAQ and watch our 3-minute promotional video.
